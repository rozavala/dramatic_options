## 1. Channel provenance tags (from day one)

Every screened candidate is tagged with its source channel:
`network` · `x_lists` · `trade_press` · `agency` · `orphan_watch` · `operator_direct`.
Tags ride the screening records (and any staging/register draft derived from them).

**Per-channel activation dates are recorded as each channel goes live** (channels start on
different dates; raw counts at a fixed checkpoint would penalize late starters).

**Dated checkpoint: 2026-09-30** — read candidates → screened → include-grade per channel;
prune dry channels as scoped negatives (the pond discipline applied to channels). A pruned
channel's residue gets no head start (the #110-family rule). **"Dry" is pre-stated
generically, not defined at the checkpoint:** a channel is prunable only if it has
≥ **k = 6** weeks of operation AND zero screen-worthy candidates (one value for all
channels). **ALL per-channel clocks — prune AND yield — run from the channel's ACTIVATION
date, never from charter ratification** (else a channel burns runway on an empty list before
it exists). A channel below k weeks at 09-30 carries to a dated second checkpoint rather
than being read early — **09-30 is a LOOK, not a kill**; any channel activated after
~mid-August reads "too young to prune" there by construction, and that is acceptable.

## 2. X practitioner lists — the pinned disciplines + the yield definition

Shape: operator-curated trade-vertical practitioner lists (choosing WHO is the human curation
act, the pond-naming family); ingestion engagement-blind and chronological.

- **X is a pointer, never evidence** — a thesis traces to verifiable prints (filings, agency
  data, tape), never to a post.
- **No engagement math anywhere** — not ranking, not filtering, not inverted as a quietness
  proxy (the CGS §8 seam violation in new clothes).
- **No LLM thesis synthesis from the stream** — v1 is a reading queue. Extraction, if ever,
  is a dated amendment behind its own small pre-reg (the no-LLM-NER door discipline).
- **"Proved yield" is defined BEFORE manual v0 starts** (anti-HARK): the lists have proved
  yield iff ≥ **1** screen-worthy candidate traceable to `x_lists` per **6** weeks **of
  operation from the channel's activation date** (the list-assembly date, recorded per §1),
  read at the §1 checkpoint. API ingestion is built only after (a) this bar is met on the
  manual v0 AND (b) a live check that the operator's API tier covers list-timeline reads at
  the needed volume.

## 3. The digest (weekly, one file, many feeds)

Feeds: trade-press RSS (non-financial salience axis) · agency/program milestone feeds ·
post-IPO orphan watch · x_lists (manual v0). **The orphan watch v0 = IPO cohort aged 12+
months × newly-listed options class ONLY — both pure existence/expression events.** A
"decayed coverage" leg would be inverted-salience math — computing quietness, the exact
operation §2 forbids — so it is excluded by construction; the quietness read on the surfaced
cohort stays where it always was: the operator and the council's news axis.

**The digest must not rank: chronological or source-grouped ordering ONLY; no scoring field
exists anywhere in the harness schema** — ordering is ranking in disguise; the door is closed
before it is tempting. **Overflow is pre-decided and salience-blind:** if a week's volume
exceeds reading capacity, the policy is per-source item caps with chronological truncation —
never a relevance filter (ranking through the back door, under deadline). The correct durable
fix for persistent overflow is tightening the human curation of which feeds/lists are in (the
pond-naming act again), not smarter filtering.

## 3b. The survivor-card compression + the per-THESIS provenance guard (advisor round 5, adopted)

The weekly unit delivered to the operator is not the raw item list but the **survivor card**:
ticker-bearing item → deterministic feasibility screen (price/ADV/optionability/band-fit,
pass/fail) → automated premise-currency pull (the Rule-5 stage, in-pipeline) → draft thesis
with falsifier attached. **Compression happens ONLY by sanctioned deterministic steps —
never by ranking**; survivors are listed alphabetically or chronologically.

**Every thesis carries a provenance tag** — `machine_surfaced_machine_drafted` vs
`operator_sourced` — from draft through admission and resolution. The guard it buys: if 100%
of flow becomes machine-surfaced and machine-framed, operator validation risks becoming
confirmation of the machine's frame — the tag makes that an EMPIRICAL question (yield quality
by provenance, readable at the first resolutions) instead of a philosophical one.

## 4. Supply-chain mapper v0 — pre-registered question + the NER door

v0 is a **measurement instrument about its own channel** before it is a tool. Structural
sources ONLY: Exhibit 21 subsidiaries; XBRL segment/geography revenue; customer-concentration
tags (which give *that* a >10% customer exists, frequently not *who*). Named counterparties in
risk-factor/MD&A prose are the LLM-NER door and are OUT of v0.

**Pinned v0 question (read at a dated point after first use):** what fraction of ruled themes
yields ≥1 *named* counterparty candidate from structural sources alone?

**Pre-commitment:** a disappointing v0 fraction is a SCOPED NEGATIVE about structural-source
reach — it does NOT open the NER door by reflex. The NER variant opens only on its own
standalone thesis + small pre-reg (bounded, grounding-only, never ranks) — §21 lesson 2
applied prospectively instead of learned again.
